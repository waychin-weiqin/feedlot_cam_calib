\section{Results}
\label{sec:results}

\subsection{Experimental Setup}

\paragraph{Dataset.} We evaluate on 8 static cameras (2688$\times$1520 resolution) deployed in a cattle feedlot. We sample $N_t = 91$ timesteps at regular intervals from synchronised video. Ground truth poses are obtained from COLMAP applied to a static capture of the same scene (without cattle).

\paragraph{Evaluation metrics.} All methods are evaluated after Sim(3) Procrustes alignment to ground truth (with reflection check):
\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{ATE}: Absolute Translation Error (RMSE of camera position errors)
    \item \textbf{ARE}: Absolute Rotation Error (mean geodesic distance to GT rotations, in degrees)
    \item \textbf{RPE rot / trans}: Relative Pose Error averaged over all $\binom{8}{2} = 28$ camera pairs
    \item \textbf{C2C3}: Distance between co-located cameras C2 and C3 (GT $\approx$ 0; structural consistency test)
\end{itemize}

Note that ATE and RPE trans are in arbitrary units (scene scale is determined by the Sim(3) alignment, not metric ground truth).

\subsection{Analysis}

Several patterns are evident from Table~\ref{tab:comparison}:

\paragraph{Classical post-processing helps translation, not rotation.} The pipeline (row 2) reduces ATE by 10\% (pose graph with co-location constraints) but provides no rotation improvement. This confirms that the rotation error in Pi3X's output is systematic bias, not statistical noise amenable to averaging.

\paragraph{Self-conditioning improves rotation at the cost of translation.} Na\"ive conditioning (row 3) achieves a 20\% ARE reduction but increases ATE by 3\%. This asymmetry motivates the decoupled approach.

\paragraph{Hybrid fusion resolves the tradeoff.} Row 4 shows that by taking rotations from the conditioned run and translations from the unconditioned run, we achieve rotation improvement (3.45\degree{}) while preserving---and slightly improving---translation (ATE = 0.159). At only 2$\times$ the compute cost, this is the most practical configuration.

\paragraph{MC iteration pushes rotation further.} The full method (row 5) gains an additional 9\% rotation improvement over single-step hybrid fusion (3.45\degree{} $\rightarrow$ 3.13\degree{}), at the cost of 13$\times$ total compute. This may be justified for high-precision applications but is not necessary for most practical deployments.

\paragraph{Overall improvement.} Comparing row 1 (raw model output) to row 5 (full method): ARE improves by 28\% (4.37\degree{} $\rightarrow$ 3.13\degree{}), RPE rotation by 27\%, and RPE translation by 21\%, while ATE improves by 10\%. The co-location distance drops from 0.088 to exactly 0.000 (hard constraint).
